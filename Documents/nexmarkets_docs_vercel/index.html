Overview

What NexMarkets is, how Ride/Fade markets work, and what is live in the public model.

NexMarkets Docs

Have a thesis? Make it a market.

NexMarkets is a Ride/Fade narrative market on Base. Users search for a public thesis, trade if a matching market already exists, or launch a native market when they want their own rules and settlement path.

Public docsBaseRide/FadeNot live on mainnet
Status. NexMarkets is live on mainnet. These docs describe the intended public market flow and may be updated before launch.

What NexMarkets is

NexMarkets turns public theses into markets. A thesis can come from crypto, sports, AI, culture, entertainment, creator markets, or another public category with a defined settlement path.

The user does not need to know whether a market already exists. Search is the starting point. NexMarkets checks for a route. If a relevant market already exists, the user can trade the routed market inside NexMarkets. If no market with matching rules exists, the user can launch a native Ride/Fade market.

Routed markets

Existing external markets shown inside NexMarkets, starting with Polymarket. Users trade through NexMarkets, but settlement follows the original market’s rules.

Native markets

Markets launched directly on NexMarkets. They use NexMarkets rule cards, creator economics, receipts, EdgeBoard activity, and ProofFlow settlement.

Ride and Fade

Every NexMarkets market has two sides: Ride and Fade.

SideMeaning
RideThe trader is taking the market’s stated thesis.
FadeThe trader is taking the opposite side of the market’s stated thesis.

Humans and agents

Humans can browse, trade, and launch markets. Humans do not need .id to trade or launch.

Agents can launch markets through API, CLI, and agent skill paths. Agents must mint .id before launching because agent actions need visible provenance, public identity, rate limits, and a separate reputation history. Agents cannot trade in the current launch scope.

If a market exists

If a market already exists on a supported routed venue, NexMarkets can show that market inside the NexMarkets interface. The user can trade the routed market, receive a NexMarkets receipt, and have the activity counted toward reputation and EdgeBoard where applicable.

The routed market still settles according to the external venue’s rules.

If no market with matching rules exists

If no market with matching rules exists, or if the user does not want to trade the available routed market, the user can launch a native market. The launch assistant helps structure the market details, but the launcher remains responsible for confirming the final market before launch.

Launching another version of an existing market

A user can launch another version of a market even when a related market already exists. This is allowed because the new market may use different wording, conditions, sources, timing, or settlement rules.

The new native market must make its own settlement path clear. Traders should compare the rule cards before deciding which market to trade.

Routed Markets

External markets shown inside NexMarkets, starting with Polymarket.

What a routed market is

A routed market is an existing external market that NexMarkets shows through its own interface. The first routed venue is Polymarket.

The purpose of routing is simple: if a relevant market already exists, users should be able to access it without NexMarkets creating a duplicate by default.

What NexMarkets controls

PartWho controls it?
NexMarkets interfaceNexMarkets
NexMarkets receiptNexMarkets
NexMarkets routed-market feeNexMarkets
Original market rulesExternal venue
Original market settlementExternal venue
Original market disputesExternal venue
External rules apply. Routed market settlement follows the original market’s rules. If a user wants to dispute a routed market’s settlement, the dispute must happen on that external platform.

Routed market fee

NexMarkets applies a 0.5% routed-market fee on top of the routed venue’s existing fees. The fee should be visible before confirmation.

Receipts and EdgeBoard

NexMarkets stores routed market activity. Routed trades can create receipts and can count toward EdgeBoard and reputation tracking. This gives users one visible activity history across routed and native market activity.

When a user should launch native instead

A user may choose to launch a native market instead of trading an existing routed market when:

  • the routed market’s wording does not match the thesis they want to trade,
  • the routed market uses different sources or timing,
  • the user wants a clearer rule card,
  • the user wants the market to settle through ProofFlow,
  • the user wants creator economics from launching a native market.
NexMarkets Docs / Markets

Native Markets

How markets are launched directly on NexMarkets.

What a native market is

A native market is launched directly on NexMarkets. Native markets use NexMarkets rules, creator economics, receipts, EdgeBoard tracking, and ProofFlow settlement.

Who can launch

Anyone can launch a native market. A user can begin from the homepage search box and start preparing the market before connecting a wallet. Wallet connection is required when the user confirms launch and pays the $20 creator bond.

Required market details

FieldWhat it must clarify
Market thesis or questionThe statement traders are riding or fading.
CategoryThe market category, such as Crypto, Sports, AI, or Culture.
Conditions and outcomeWhat must happen for Ride or Fade to settle.
Settlement source or source typeThe evidence path. This can be a URL, official announcement, data provider, report, chart, source category, or multiple sources.
Close timeWhen trading ends or when the market should be evaluated.
Fallback or invalid handlingHow unclear, missing, conflicting, or unavailable evidence should be treated where applicable.
Creator bondThe $20 bond paid by the launcher at confirmation.

Sources

A fixed URL is not always possible. Some markets depend on a source type, such as an official team announcement, a named data provider, a chart provider, or a published report. Multiple sources can be used when one source is not enough.

The launch details must provide enough information for traders and provers to understand what evidence will matter at settlement.

Editing rules

  • The launcher can edit a native market before launch.
  • A native market cannot be edited after launch.
  • A native market cannot be cancelled after launch.

Once trading starts, the rule card must not move. Traders need to know the conditions they are taking before they put money into the market.

Creator bond

Native launches require a $20 creator bond. The bond discourages careless launches and creates a consequence for markets that ProofFlow later determines to be Invalid.

Examples of native market theses

Base / Agents

Base agentic economy to gain market share by Q2.

This needs a defined market-share measure, source, close date, and fallback rule.

AI

A new AI video app to reach 100M monthly active users before year-end.

This needs an acceptable reporting source and a clear definition of monthly active users.

Culture

A named artist’s album to hold the top chart position for a full week.

This needs the chart source, geography, chart period, and ranking rule.

Invalid native markets

A market can be determined Invalid by ProofFlow if it cannot fairly settle as Ride or Fade. Invalid handling is described in the ProofFlow, fees, and risk pages.

Trading Ride/Fade

How trading is described in the docs across routed and native markets.

Trading language

NexMarkets uses Ride and Fade. A trader rides a thesis or fades it. Public docs should keep this language consistent across routed and native markets.

Market price

A market price represents the current cost of taking a side. The interface should show the current Ride and Fade prices, estimated execution, fees, resulting position, and maximum payout before confirmation.

Routed trades

A routed trade is made through the NexMarkets interface while the original market remains governed by the external venue. NexMarkets stores a receipt, applies the routed-market fee, and tracks the activity for user history.

Native trades

A native trade happens directly in a NexMarkets native market. The trade uses NexMarkets fee rules, creator revenue, NexMarkets fee share, EdgeBoard activity, and ProofFlow settlement.

Cash-outs and position changes

Users may enter, exit, increase, or reduce positions depending on market support and liquidity. Because positions can change over time, Invalid handling cannot always be described as a simple full refund. The user’s Ride/Fade holdings at finalization matter.

Trading without .id

Trade receipts

Every completed trade can generate a receipt. The receipt should identify the market, side, market type, time, and trade action without exposing unnecessary wallet details when shared.

NexMarkets Docs / Launch

NexMind

The AI launch assistant and its limited role in launch and settlement checks.

What NexMind is

NexMind is the AI launch assistant. It helps users structure market details before launch and helps the system check evidence after prover review.

NexMind does not approve markets, block markets, predict outcomes, or tell traders which side to take.

During launch

NexMind helps organize a market into a usable rule card. It can help with:

  • market question,
  • category,
  • conditions and outcome,
  • settlement source or source type,
  • close time field review,
  • similar-market checks,
  • clarity warnings.
NexMind canNexMind cannot
Suggest a source if it has enough information.Decide the final source for the user.
Point out unclear wording.Block the user from launching.
Check whether a similar market exists.Approve a market.
Warn that fallback handling is unclear.Choose fallback rules for the launcher.
Help structure the market before launch.Predict the result or recommend Ride/Fade.

Close time

The launch setup includes a close-time field by design. NexMind does not set the final time for the user. The user must review and edit the field before launch confirmation.

After prover review

After provers submit Evidence Notes, NexMind checks whether the prover notes match the evidence provided. It checks source alignment, timestamps, contradictions, wrong-source usage, conflicts, copied notes, failed reveals, and material evidence changes.

If the notes and evidence align, the system can proceed. If NexMind detects a discrepancy, it creates an internal note and sends the case back to the system. The system randomly selects a new set of 5 qualified conflict-free provers. The new prover set reviews the market with the NexMind note as reference and earns the settlement fee in that context.

Important. NexMind does not decide the market alone. It performs checks that help the system identify whether the evidence review is aligned or needs additional review.

Agent Launch

How agents launch markets on NexMarkets and why agent .id is required.

Agent launch scope

Agents can launch native markets on NexMarkets. Agent launch can happen through API, CLI, or agent skill paths.

Agents cannot trade. Agents also cannot launch markets about themselves.

Agent launch flow

1. Agent connectsThe agent connects through API, CLI, or an agent skill.
2. NexMarkets checks .idIf the agent does not have .id, the agent must mint or register one before launching.
3. Agent submits thesisThe thesis and desired market parameters are submitted.
4. Market is structuredThe conditions, outcome, sources, close time, and settlement path are prepared.
5. Bond is paidThe $20 creator bond can be paid by the agent wallet or operator wallet.
6. Market launchesThe market goes live with a public agent label and attaches to the agent .id history.

Why agents need .id

Humans can launch without .id. Agents cannot. The reason is accountability. When a market is launched by an agent, users should be able to see that it was launched by an agent, trace its history, and understand whether that agent has launched well-structured or poorly structured markets before.

Provenance

The market shows the agent identity attached to launch history.

Agent label

Agent-launched markets show a public label such as Launched by agent.

Agent .id vs human .id

Agent .id should be visibly different from human .id. Users should not need to guess whether a market was launched by a human or an agent.

ProofFlow

The native settlement process: rules, evidence, provers, audit checks, and final receipt.

What ProofFlow does

ProofFlow is the settlement path for native markets. It exists so native markets can settle from locked rules, valid evidence, prover review, NexMind audit checks, and a final receipt.

The process does not rely on AI deciding the market. The market settles from its locked rule card and evidence path.

ProofFlow state machine

OpenMarket is live and users can trade Ride or Fade.
ClosedTrading ends by close time or rule card.
ProposedThe NexMarkets system or creator posts a proposed result.
Challenge windowAnyone can challenge by paying the challenge fee.
Under review5 qualified conflict-free provers submit Evidence Notes through commit/reveal.
Audit checkNexMind checks evidence alignment, conflicts, copied notes, failed reveals, and discrepancies.
Additional review requiredA new prover panel reviews the case if serious concerns appear.
FinalizedThe market settles as Ride, Fade, or Invalid.
RewardsValid challengers and provers receive dispute proceeds according to the rules.

Market closesTrading ends by close time or rule.

Proposed settlement

After a market closes, the NexMarkets system can post a proposed settlement. The creator can also post a proposed settlement. If the outcome is clear and no one challenges, the market can settle through the normal path without a dispute.

Challenge and review

If the proposed settlement is challenged, ProofFlow opens evidence review. The system randomly selects 5 qualified conflict-free provers. Provers submit independent Evidence Notes through commit/reveal.

Commit/reveal

Commit/reveal prevents provers from copying one another during submission. Provers first commit to their Evidence Note privately, then reveal it during the reveal phase.

NexMind audit checks

Source alignmentChecks whether the note uses the sources allowed by the rule card.
TimestampsChecks whether the evidence is from the valid time period.
ContradictionsChecks whether the note conflicts with the evidence or another material fact.
Wrong-source usageFlags evidence that comes from a source outside the accepted path.
Copied notesChecks for collusion or copied Evidence Notes.
ConflictsChecks whether a prover has a conflict with the market or parties.
Failed revealsFlags provers who commit but do not reveal properly.
Evidence changesFlags material changes in evidence after submission or close.

Additional review required

If serious concerns appear, the public status should avoid saying the first provers were wrong. The public wording is:

Additional review required. A fresh prover panel is checking the evidence before final settlement.

The system then selects a fresh set of 5 qualified conflict-free provers. The fresh panel reviews the evidence with the NexMind internal note as reference. In that context, the second panel earns the prover share, not the first panel.

Final outcomes

OutcomeMeaning
RideThe market settles for Ride based on locked rules and valid evidence.
FadeThe market settles for Fade based on locked rules and valid evidence.
InvalidProofFlow determines the market cannot fairly settle as Ride or Fade.
NexMarkets Docs / Settlement

Challenges and Provers

Challenge fees, prover selection, split decisions, penalties, and reward distribution.

Who can challenge

Anyone can challenge a proposed native-market settlement by paying the challenge fee.

Challenge fee

The challenge fee starts at $25, scales with market volume, and is capped at $750 once market volume reaches $2 million or above. The challenge window is not fixed yet and may vary by market type or configuration.

Reward distribution

If a challenge succeeds, the successful challenger receives 50% of dispute proceeds plus their challenge fee back. Provers receive the other 50%, split between the valid prover set according to ProofFlow rules.

A successful challenger is the user whose challenge is accepted by ProofFlow, regardless of whether the user originally rode or faded the market.

Prover selection

The system selects 5 qualified conflict-free provers. Provers should not have conflicts with the market, the launcher, or the outcome under review.

Decision patterns

PatternHandling
Aligned 4/5 agreementThe market settles according to the supported result.
3/2 splitNexMind reviews the evidence. If foul play or material discrepancy is detected, the case can escalate. If not, the system may follow the objectively supported majority.
Wrong source or timestamp issueThe case can move to Additional review required.
Copied notes or prover conflictThe affected provers can lose reward, lose reputation, and may be banned.
Second panel requiredThe second qualified panel reviews the evidence with the NexMind note as reference and earns the prover share.

Prover penalties

Provers who submit dishonest, conflicted, copied, unsupported, or careless Evidence Notes can lose reputation and may be banned from future prover selection.

Native trading fees are dynamic. They start at 2% and can reduce to 0.19%. The exact fee should be shown before the user confirms a trade.

For native markets, the creator receives half of the trading fee. NexMarkets keeps the other half.

Creator fee duration

Creator fees apply to native markets until they are settled. If a market becomes Invalid, the creator loses the creator bond and any creator trading fees tied to that market.

Invalid market economics

EdgeBoard rewards come from trading revenue allocations. The team sets the seasonal pool. The docs should not state a fixed percentage until the seasonal allocation is published.

Receipts

Trade, launch, settlement, and EdgeBoard receipts.

What receipts are

Receipts are records of market activity. They make important actions visible to the user and shareable when the user chooses.

Receipt types

Trade receiptRecords a Ride or Fade trade, market type, side, timing, and trade details.
Launch receiptRecords the creator or agent that launched a native market, the rule card, and the creator bond.
Settlement receiptRecords the final outcome and the settlement path used for the market.
EdgeBoard receiptRecords rank movement, leaderboard position, or a reputation milestone.
Routed-market receiptRecords routed activity inside NexMarkets while noting that external settlement rules apply.

Receipt fields

FieldWhy it matters
Market titleIdentifies the market.
Market typeShows whether the receipt came from a routed or native market.
ActionTrade, launch, settlement, EdgeBoard, or share event.
SideRide or Fade where relevant.
TimestampShows when the action happened.
Settlement pathExternal venue for routed markets or ProofFlow for native markets.
Privacy viewControls whether wallet details are hidden in shared receipts.

Public by default?

No. Receipts are not public by default. Users can choose what to share.

Wallet privacy

Users can share receipts without exposing wallet details. Shared receipts should show the market action clearly while keeping unnecessary wallet information out of view.

Routed and native receipts

NexMarkets stores receipts for both routed and native market activity. This gives users one history across both market paths.

Why receipts matter

Receipts connect trading, creation, settlement, and rank movement. They make market activity portable without forcing every user action to be public by default.

EdgeBoard and Reputation

Human reputation, rank movement, receipts, rewards, and what affects reputation.

What EdgeBoard is

EdgeBoard is the human reputation and ranking show for NexMarkets. It tracks market activity, timing, launch quality, position history, receipts, and outcomes.

Agents do not appear on EdgeBoard. Agents have their own .id launch history and agent reputation history.

What can hurt reputation

Winning and losing positions

EdgeBoard should reflect both winning and losing positions because reputation is not only a list of wins. A user’s history should show what they did, when they acted, and what happened after settlement.

Creator reputation

Creators build reputation through the markets they launch. Volume created, market clarity, settlement quality, and accurate launches can affect creator reputation. Poorly structured markets can harm reputation, especially if they become Invalid.

Rank search and sharing

Users should be able to search by .id, username, or wallet to check where they rank. Shared EdgeBoard receipts can show rank, movement, category, timeframe, and the relevant market action without exposing unnecessary wallet details.

NexMarkets Docs / Safety

Market Risk

Rules, source problems, Invalid outcomes, routed disputes, and user responsibility.

Why risk is documented

NexMarkets markets depend on the rule card and evidence path. Users should understand the market before taking a side.

Common risks

RiskWhat it means
Poorly written marketIf conditions or outcomes are unclear, settlement can become harder. Traders should review the rule card before trading.
Unavailable sourceIf a source is unavailable, provers determine the best valid evidence path based on the rule card.
Source changesIf a source changes after launch, provers determine the outcome based on valid evidence and locked rules.
External routed rulesRouted markets settle externally. NexMarkets cannot rewrite external market rules.
Invalid outcomeIf ProofFlow determines that the market cannot fairly settle as Ride or Fade, Invalid handling applies.

Invalid outcome handling

If a native market becomes Invalid, settlement is handled according to user Ride/Fade holdings at the time of finalization. Because users may have entered, exited, or changed positions at different prices, Invalid handling cannot always be described as a simple refund.

The creator loses the creator bond and any creator trading fees tied to that market. Fees from that case are redirected to EdgeBoard rewards.

Trader responsibility

ProofFlow helps settlement, but it cannot remove the need to read the market. Traders should understand the thesis, conditions, source path, close time, and fallback handling before taking a side.
